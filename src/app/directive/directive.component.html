Directives

It help us to manupulate and change the behaviour of DOM elements.
Their are following 4 types of directives.
  1. Component Directive
  2. Structural Directive
  3. Attribute Directive
  4. Custom Directive

1. Component Directive

Component directives are based on component. Actually, each component can be used as directive. Component provides @Input and @Output decorator to send and receive information between parent and child components.

2. Structural Directive

Structural directives change the structure of DOM by adding or removing elements. It is denoted by * sign with three pre-defined directives NgIf, NgFor and NgSwitch.

  1. *NgIf Directive
  2. *NgFor Directive
  3. *NgSwitch Directive

2a. *NgIf Directive

NgIf directive is used to display or hide data in your application based on the condition becomes true or false.

Attribute directives performs the appearance or behavior of DOM elements or components. Some of the examples are NgStyle ang NgClass.

  2. NgClass

  • syntax -> [ngStyle]= {{ '"{property_name:value,property_name:value,...n}"' }}
